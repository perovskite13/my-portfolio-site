About

Joanne enjoys gaining diverse experiences and exploring passions ranging from coding to music.

Holds a master in Environmental Science, she aspires to be a technical programmer with a strong data science and analytical mindset.

Throughout her career journey, she has developed her technical and analytical skillsets spanning from lake modelling with CSIRO Black Mountain laboratory in Canberra, hydrogeochemical modelling of mine pit lake, large-scale environmental monitoring and hydrogeological investigations for various industries, to now, supply chain and operational analytics. She loves working in the scientific modelling and analytics space, and is eager to expand her coding technicality along the journey.

She was also part of the Robogals' committee board of directors, a non-for-profit organisation that promotes science and engineering to young girls. In her free time, you can find her playing games, fangirling over movies and video game soundtracks.

Joanne is also a harpist, pianist and arranger who strives to convey her music interpretations through her harp, piano and audio production skills. Constantly inspired by video game and film music, she likes experimenting with soundscape, synths and ambience. Since 2015, Joanne has worked in various projects via remote collaboration, drawing on her arranging, performance and music technology work experiences. She is passionate about improving her harp musicianship and providing real harp for an immersive soundtrack experience.
